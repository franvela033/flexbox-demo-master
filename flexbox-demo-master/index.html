Francisco Velazquez

Atlanta based multimedia bilingual journalist, writer and fitness fan.

Francisco is a multimedia journalist born and raised in Atlanta, Georgia. He is a contributing reporter for Borderless Magazine, a publication focused on immigration journalism in Chicago, Illinois. Velazquez recieved his bachelor's degree in Journalism and Anthropology from the University of Wisconsin-Madison, including a certificate in Chicano/Latino Studies. He is working towards his master's degree in broadcast journalism at the Craig Newmark Graduate School of Journalism.

Velazquez has interned at Harvard's Immigration and Refugee Clinic and has worked as a Spanish interpreter at the South Texas Family Residential Center in Dilley, Texas, the largest immigrant detention center housing women and children in the U.S. He has prepared women for their credible fear interviews (CFI) and translated for them in front of an asylum judge. Velazquez has done similar work with male and LGBTQ+ detainees in Lumpkin, Georgia, where he also assisted in translating affidavits and attending courtroom bond hearings.

Velazquez is an occasional poet and crime junkie fanatic.

His past work includes issues of immigration, gender and sexuality, government and arts and culture. His most recent work also features spanish translations.
